# Contributor License Agreement (CLA)

## Why a CLA?

A Contributor License Agreement ensures that Desygn AI has the necessary rights to use and distribute contributions, while protecting contributors by making the terms of contribution explicit. It also protects users of the project by ensuring clear provenance of all code.

- **License grant**: You grant Desygn AI a perpetual, worldwide, non-exclusive, no-charge, royalty-free, irrevocable license to reproduce, prepare derivative works of, publicly display, publicly perform, sublicense, and distribute your contributions and such derivative works.
- **Right to submit**: You have the legal right to grant the above license. If your employer has rights to intellectual property you create, you have received permission to submit the contribution on behalf of that employer, or your employer has waived such rights for your contributions to this project.
- **Public record**: You understand that your contribution and this agreement are public and that a record of the contribution (including all personal information you submit with it) is maintained indefinitely and may be redistributed consistent with the project license or applicable laws.
- **No warranty**: You provide your contributions on an "as is" basis, without warranties or conditions of any kind, either express or implied.

## How to Sign

To sign this CLA, choose one of the following methods:

1. **PR comment**: Add a comment on your first pull request with the exact text:
   ```
   I agree to the CLA
   ```
2. **Signed commit**: Include the following in your commit message using `git commit -s`, which appends a `Signed-off-by` line.

You only need to sign once. Subsequent contributions from the same GitHub account are covered automatically.

## Corporate CLA

If you are making contributions on behalf of a company or organization (i.e., if your contributions are owned by your employer), a Corporate CLA is required in addition to the Individual CLA.

To initiate a Corporate CLA, please contact the maintainers by opening an issue labeled `corporate-cla` or emailing the project maintainers. The Corporate CLA grants the same license on behalf of the organization for all designated employees.
